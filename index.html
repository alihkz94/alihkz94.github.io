About Me

Ph.D. candidate in Ecology and Botany at University of Tartu, Estonia, specializing in bioinformatics analysis of long DNA markers with focus on microbiome and fungal eDNA barcoding, metabarcoding, and metagenomics.

My research combines cutting-edge computational methods with molecular biology to advance our understanding of microbial ecosystems. I have extensive experience in developing and implementing bioinformatics pipelines, particularly for metabarcoding data analysis through my work on PipeCraft2.

Research Focus

Metabarcoding & Metagenomics

Developing bioinformatics pipelines for microbiome and fungal eDNA analysis, including contributions to PipeCraft2 and EUKARYOME database.

Long-Read Amplicon Analysis

Evaluation and enhancement of chimera detection algorithms on long-read amplicons using PacBio and Oxford Nanopore technologies.

Single-Cell Analysis

Transcriptomics

Environmental Genomics

Pipeline Development
