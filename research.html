Science & Inquiry

Research

Six interconnected areas of investigation — spanning ecology, genomics, and computational biology.

01

Metabarcoding & Metagenomics

Environmental DNA profiling of microbial and eukaryotic communities using amplicon-based and shotgun sequencing approaches. Core work involves PipeCraft2 — a fully containerized, Nextflow-based pipeline supporting end-to-end metabarcoding analysis from raw reads to community tables. The EUKARYOME database provides the taxonomic backbone for identification across all eukaryotic domains, enabling precision environmental monitoring and biodiversity assessment.

PipeCraft2 EUKARYOME eDNA Amplicon Sequencing Community Ecology
02

Long-Read Amplicon Analysis

Systematic benchmarking of chimera detection algorithms for PacBio and Oxford Nanopore long-read amplicons, resolving which approaches minimize false chimeras while preserving true biological signal across different marker genes and community diversities.

PacBio Nanopore Chimera Detection QC
03

Single-Cell Analysis

scRNA-seq and CROP-seq workflows for cancer research, integrating CRISPR screens with transcriptional profiling. Applied ML frameworks (scVI, TensorFlow, PyTorch) for cell-type annotation, trajectory inference, CNV detection, and clonal evolution modeling at single-cell resolution.

scRNA-seq CROP-seq CRISPR scVI ML
04

Transcriptomics

Differential expression analysis using DESeq2, edgeR, and MAST, alongside development of the DEASS statistical method — designed to improve power and reduce false discovery in complex multi-group RNA-seq experimental designs.

RNA-seq DESeq2 edgeR DEASS GSEA
05

Environmental Genomics

The TREC (Traversing European Coastlines) project is a large-scale international initiative sampling marine and coastal environments across Europe to characterize microbial and eukaryotic biodiversity using metabarcoding and metagenomics. This work spans multiple countries, sequencing platforms, and bioinformatic workflows to build a continental-scale picture of coastal ecosystem health and community dynamics.

TREC Project Coastal Ecology Biodiversity Metabarcoding International Collaboration
06

Pipeline Development

Designing reproducible, containerized bioinformatics workflows using Nextflow and Snakemake with Docker and Singularity. Deployable on HPC clusters (SLURM) and cloud (AWS), ensuring portability and scalability across research environments.

Nextflow Snakemake Docker SLURM AWS
